Educational Disclaimer

NutriScope is an educational planning tool, not a medical or clinical nutrition device. All calorie and nutrient figures are approximate averages drawn from standard food composition references for common portion sizes — actual values vary by brand, preparation method, ripeness, and regional variety. BMR, calorie, and RDA-style targets use widely accepted population formulas (Mifflin-St Jeor for energy, general adult RDAs for nutrients) and are not personalized medical prescriptions. Nothing in this app should be used to diagnose, treat, or manage any health condition, including diabetes, anemia, kidney disease, or eating disorders. Always consult a registered dietitian, physician, or other qualified healthcare professional before making significant changes to your diet, especially if you are pregnant, nursing, managing a medical condition, or taking medication that interacts with food (e.g. blood thinners and Vitamin K, or MAOIs and tyramine). All data you enter stays in your browser session and is not transmitted anywhere.

Nutrition Data Sources

General references used to approximate the values in this app's food database
Indian Food Composition Tables (IFCT) — ICMR–National Institute of Nutrition
Primary reference for Indian staples, dals, millets, and regional dishes such as idli, dosa, and poha.
USDA FoodData Central
Cross-reference for fruits, vegetables, nuts, seeds, dairy, and packaged staples.
ICMR–NIN Nutrient Requirements for Indians (RDA/EAR guidelines)
Basis for the simplified daily targets used for protein, iron, calcium, and vitamin recommendations.
Mifflin-St Jeor Equation
Basal metabolic rate formula used to estimate energy needs from age, height, weight, and gender.
NutriScope runs entirely in your browser — no data leaves your device. Nutrient values are approximate averages for planning purposes, not medical advice.
